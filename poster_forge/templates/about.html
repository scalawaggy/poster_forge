About Poster Forge

Version 3.1.0-build.42 (Sub-Mariner Edition)

Poster Forge is an automated multi-function media utility that bridges the gap between your *arr instances and Plex. It manages poster acquisition, dynamically generates and paints custom countdown banners for upcoming media, and performs regular maintenance jobs to keep your libraries pristine.

Core Integrations & Requirements

To fully utilize the Automated Tracking Engine, you must configure your external dependencies properly.

1. Plex Configuration

  • You MUST create a dedicated Movie Library in Plex and name it exactly Coming Soon. You must also point it to a valid, writable folder on your hard drive.
  • The app will automatically drop downloaded trailers into this folder and group them into a Coming Soon collection. You must manually change its collection order to custom so the app can reorder the items dynamically.
  • The app will also automatically create a Returning Soon collection for TV shows that already exist on your server but are going into a new season. You must manually change its collection order to custom as well.
  • Plex Home Screen Visibility: Plex does not automatically pin collections to your global home screen. To see the "Coming Soon" or "Returning Soon" hubs when you open Plex, navigate to the collection inside your library, edit it, go to the Advanced tab, and change the Visible on setting to Library/Home/Friend's Home.
  • Ensure your Poster Forge Docker container has mounted your exact Plex Media directories as defined in Settings.

2. Radarr & Sonarr (The *arrs)

  • Poster Forge connects directly to their APIs to identify upcoming releases.
  • Because new movies and unreleased TV shows do not exist on your hard drive yet, Plex cannot normally display them. Poster Forge bypasses this by physically downloading the latest high-quality trailer for the upcoming item and injecting it into Plex as a "fake" movie!
  • The system will track movies that have a status of announced or inCinemas, and shows with upcoming episode air dates.

3. Tautulli (The Handoff)

  • The instant an item finally hits your library and is processed by Plex, Tautulli fires a webhook to Poster Forge.
  • Poster Forge will immediately act as an assassin, removing the "Coming Soon" or "Returning Soon" banners, applying a clean poster, and—if it was a movie—automatically deleting the fake trailer from your hard drive!
  • Webhook Setup:
    1. In Tautulli, go to Settings > Notification Agents > Add a Webhook.
    2. Webhook URL: http://[POSTER_FORGE_IP]:8000/api/webhook/tautulli?token=YOUR_SECURE_TOKEN
    3. Triggers: Select Recently Added.
    4. Data (JSON): 
      {
    "action": "added",
    "ratingKey": "{rating_key}",
    "mediaType": "{media_type}",
    "title": "{title}"
}

Security Note

Ensure you configure a Webhook Security Token in the Settings panel. Leaving it blank exposes your webhook endpoints to unauthorized access. Poster Forge utilizes safe path translations, but running the container behind a reverse proxy (like Nginx or Traefik) without basic auth is not recommended for external facing networks.
